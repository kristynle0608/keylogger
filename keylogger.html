Keylogger

What is a Keylogger?

A keylogger is a malware that secretly monitors user inputs into a device by analyzing keystrokes. A keylogger is also a form of spyware, a type of malware that is used to gather information about a user or an organization.

Is a Keylogger Dangerous?

Yes, a keylogger can be dangerous.
If a keylogger is downloaded into your device, hackers can use the malware to monitor what you type via your keyboard. Whenever you typed private information using your device, the hacker can get them and they can use them to their leverage. For example, they are able to take your money since they can monitor what you typed whenever you access your banking information online.

How to Protect Your Device from Keyloggers

- Avoid clicking on suspicious links and downloading unauthorized software.
- If you are downloading an antivirus software, verify if it's a legitimate one.
- Educate yourself on the dangers of spyware and how to detect/prevent them.
